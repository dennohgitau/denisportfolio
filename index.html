About me

I am a technology leader who delivers reliable systems at scale across infrastructure, security, and software. I repeatedly turn ambiguous operational pain into clear architectures, automation, and measurable outcomes—including high-availability migrations, disaster recovery with defined RTO and RPO, major incident reduction, and platform efficiency through disciplined engineering (monitoring, runbooks, capacity planning, and infrastructure as code). I am equally comfortable shipping regulated, money-moving full-stack insurance products and hardening enterprise networks with firewall-led security models, and I collaborate effectively with business, risk, and engineering teams. I learn quickly, document decisions responsibly, and optimise for uptime, security, cost, and speed of delivery in real production environments.

At The Monarch Insurance Company, I lead development and innovation end to end: digital motor acquisition for agents and direct customers, async DMVIC certificate issuance with queue workers and tunable concurrency, multi-rail payments (including M-Pesa STK and bank verification flows), idempotent payment state at the database layer, and integration with enterprise Oracle and policy-core systems—so digital channels stay fast while back-office and regulatory truth stay aligned.

As a DevOps-minded engineer, I design and run CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure as code, and automated deployments across the lifecycle, using Jenkins, GitLab CI/CD, GitHub Actions, and AWS-native tooling where it fits. I pair that operational rigor with strong application engineering in Python, Django, JavaScript, TypeScript, React, Angular, and Node.js, building interfaces and APIs that stay performant under load.

My AWS and data platform work includes architecting scalable, secure services (EC2, S3, RDS, Lambda, Glue, Redshift, QuickSight, and more), cost-aware designs, and analytics pipelines that support leadership decisions—not only dashboards, but trustworthy data paths from ingestion to insight.

In network and data-centre practice, I work with Fortinet FortiGate controls, VPNs, segmentation, VMware estates, colocation at iColo, diverse fibre paths for redundancy, and disaster recovery patterns (including vSphere Replication and tested failover) so core insurance systems meet strict availability expectations.

Whether the goal is a polished customer journey, a hardened integration with government or banking APIs, or a calmer on-call posture, I default to evidence: smoke tests, reconciliation workflows, clear runbooks, and changes that auditors and teammates can follow with confidence.
